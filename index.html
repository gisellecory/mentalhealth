England's mental health check up

By student 1753935, 8th May 2018

Instances of mental ill health have risen

An estimated 1 in 5 people had experienced a 'common mental disorder' like depression or anxiety in the past week when surveyed. This has risen from 1 in 6 in 1993 - a change of 15.5 per cent to 18.9 per cent. Anxiety (in the form of Generalised Anxiety Disorder), depression and drug dependence are the most common illnesses.

Many indidividuals go on to access mental health care. 1 million people were in contact adult mental health services as of December 2017. People often have to wait a long time for this care. Waiting times for psychological therapy vary from 16 days in Waltham Forest to 167 days in Leicester (counting waits for both first and second treatment)

How is your area doing?

Yet not all of these experiences with mental health care are positive. Patients of IAPT (Improving Access to Psychological Therapies programme, the principal way to access mental health care) are asked about their experiences, including if, on reflection, they got the help that mattered to them at all times. Throughout England, three out of four (75 per cent) said yes - so one quarter of patients without the support they needed.

Younger people, people living in deprived areas, and people with disabilities are all less likely than average to recover from their condition after psychological therapy.
